Recent Animal Extinctions
of the Last 12,000 Years

The Earth of 12,000 years ago (practically yesterday in geologic terms) was home to animals that appear ancient and alien, as if they belonged more to the age of dinosaurs than to our own: Flightless birds that were twelve feet tall, sloths the size of elephants, spike-tailed armadillo relatives that weighed as much as a Volkswagen, and giant deer with antlers spanning nearly nine feet were just a few of the creatures that recently roamed the planet.

While many factors lead to the extinction of animals during this period, known by Earth scientists as the Holocene epoch, evidence shows that human activity was often a direct contributor.
