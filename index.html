Recent Animal Extinctions
of the Last 12,000 Years

The last 12,000 years span what geologists refer to as the Holocene epoch, a time of relative climate stability that began with the receding of great ice sheets covering much of the northern hemisphere -- and also the spread of homo sapiens to continents and isolated islands around much of the planet.

While many causes lead to the extinction of animals in the Holocene, evidence shows human activity was often a direct contribtor.
